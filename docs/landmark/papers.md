# Foundational papers

> A small, ordered reading list for the first few months in the field.

## The standards papers

- **Gorgolewski et al. (2016).** *The brain imaging data structure, a format for organizing and describing outputs of neuroimaging experiments.* **Scientific Data.** — the BIDS paper. Read first.
- **Esteban et al. (2019).** *fMRIPrep: a robust preprocessing pipeline for functional MRI.* **Nature Methods.** — the BIDS-app paradigm in one paper.
- **Cieslak et al. (2021).** *QSIPrep: an integrative platform for preprocessing and reconstructing diffusion MRI.* **Nature Methods.** — the diffusion equivalent.

## Cohort / dataset papers

- **Van Essen et al. (2013).** *The WU-Minn Human Connectome Project: an overview.* **NeuroImage.** — the HCP dataset and the pipeline.
- **Casey et al. (2018).** *The Adolescent Brain Cognitive Development (ABCD) study.* **Developmental Cognitive Neuroscience.**
- **Miller et al. (2016).** *Multimodal population brain imaging in the UK Biobank prospective epidemiological study.* **Nature Neuroscience.**
- **Markello et al. (2022).** *neuromaps: structural and functional interpretation of brain maps.* **Nature Methods.** — how to relate your results to existing maps.

## Methods papers

- **Fischl (2012).** *FreeSurfer.* **NeuroImage.** — the surface-reconstruction pipeline.
- **Smith et al. (2004).** *Advances in functional and structural MR image analysis and implementation as FSL.* **NeuroImage.**
- **Tournier et al. (2019).** *MRtrix3: a fast, flexible and open software framework for medical image processing and visualisation.* **NeuroImage.**
- **Avants et al. (2011).** *A reproducible evaluation of ANTs similarity metric performance in brain image registration.* **NeuroImage.**
- **Glasser et al. (2016).** *A multi-modal parcellation of human cerebral cortex.* **Nature.** — the HCP-MMP atlas paper.
- **Schaefer et al. (2018).** *Local-global parcellation of the human cerebral cortex from intrinsic functional connectivity MRI.* **Cerebral Cortex.** — the Schaefer parcellation.

## Statistics and reproducibility papers

- **Eklund et al. (2016).** *Cluster failure: why fMRI inferences for spatial extent have inflated false-positive rates.* **PNAS.** — the cluster-correction wake-up call.
- **Botvinik-Nezer et al. (2020).** *Variability in the analysis of a single neuroimaging dataset by many teams.* **Nature.** — the same data, 70 teams, 70 answers.
- **Marek et al. (2022).** *Reproducible brain-wide association studies require thousands of individuals.* **Nature.** — why small-cohort brain-behaviour studies don't replicate.

## Deep learning in neuroimaging

- **Ronneberger et al. (2015).** *U-Net: convolutional networks for biomedical image segmentation.* **MICCAI.** — still the most-cited architecture in medical imaging.
- **Henschel et al. (2020).** *FastSurfer — a fast and accurate deep learning based neuroimaging pipeline.* **NeuroImage.** — the deep-learning recon-all.
- **Isensee et al. (2021).** *nnU-Net: a self-configuring method for deep learning-based biomedical image segmentation.* **Nature Methods.** — the baseline you must beat.

## What to read next

If you finish this list, you have a strong base. From there, the field branches by sub-discipline; the **Annual Review of Neuroscience**, **NeuroImage**, **Medical Image Analysis**, and **Imaging Neuroscience** are the journals to follow.
