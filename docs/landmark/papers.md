# Foundational papers

> A small, ordered reading list. Each entry includes the DOI for one-click access.

Citations use the AMA-style author list, year, journal, and a clickable DOI link.

## Standards & community tooling

1. **Gorgolewski KJ, Auer T, Calhoun VD, et al.** The brain imaging data structure, a format for organizing and describing outputs of neuroimaging experiments. *Sci Data.* 2016;3:160044. [doi:10.1038/sdata.2016.44](https://doi.org/10.1038/sdata.2016.44) — the BIDS paper. Read first.
2. **Esteban O, Markiewicz CJ, Blair RW, et al.** fMRIPrep: a robust preprocessing pipeline for functional MRI. *Nat Methods.* 2019;16(1):111-116. [doi:10.1038/s41592-018-0235-4](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-018-0235-4) — the BIDS-app paradigm in one paper.
3. **Cieslak M, Cook PA, He X, et al.** QSIPrep: an integrative platform for preprocessing and reconstructing diffusion MRI data. *Nat Methods.* 2021;18(7):775-778. [doi:10.1038/s41592-021-01185-5](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-021-01185-5) — the diffusion equivalent.
4. **Markiewicz CJ, Gorgolewski KJ, Feingold F, et al.** The OpenNeuro resource for sharing of neuroscience data. *eLife.* 2021;10:e71774. [doi:10.7554/eLife.71774](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.71774) — the open-data infrastructure most public BIDS datasets live on.
5. **Halchenko YO, Meyer K, Poldrack B, et al.** DataLad: distributed system for joint management of code, data, and their relationship. *J Open Source Softw.* 2021;6(63):3262. [doi:10.21105/joss.03262](https://doi.org/10.21105/joss.03262).
6. **Yarkoni T, Markiewicz CJ, de la Vega A, et al.** PyBIDS: Python tools for BIDS datasets. *J Open Source Softw.* 2019;4(40):1294. [doi:10.21105/joss.01294](https://doi.org/10.21105/joss.01294).
7. **Ciric R, Thompson WH, Lorenz R, et al.** TemplateFlow: FAIR-sharing of multi-scale, multi-species brain models. *Nat Methods.* 2022;19(12):1568-1571. [doi:10.1038/s41592-022-01681-2](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-022-01681-2).

## How to read the list

If you're new, take them in roughly this order: 1 → 2 → 14 → 16 → 19 → 8 → 26 → 30 → 38. That gives you the standards, the pipelines, the cohort, and the reproducibility lessons in about an evening.
